PART 1: Language Structure Suggestions (Grammar)

Use these target grammar structures to boost your score when discussing historical events, making deductions, or reporting facts.

Speculating (Past & Present)
  • Must have + V3: "They must have felt extreme pressure during the Space Race."
  • Might / Could be: "It might be difficult for museums to return everything."
  • Can't have + V3: "He can't have known the disastrous consequences."
Reporting Ideas & Claims
  • Claim / Argue that: "Many historians argue that the artifacts were stolen."
  • Suggest + V-ing: "I would suggest compromising by using 3D copies."
  • Admit / Deny: "Leaders rarely admit making mistakes."

PART 2: Language Structure Suggestions (Discourse)

Use these linking phrases to organize your thoughts, compare different sides of an argument, and handle complex social questions.

Expressing & Justifying Opinions
  • "From my perspective, it is absolutely essential to..."
  • "I firmly believe that..."
  • "The main justification for this is that..."
  • "Taking responsibility means..." (Useful for Card 2)
Comparing, Contrasting & Evaluating
  • "While it is true that [A], we must also consider [B]..."
  • "On the one hand... On the other hand..."
  • "Instead of getting defensive, it's better to..."
  • "Compared to the past, today we..."
